# Audio credits

## `mark-bra-perfect.wav` / `mark-bra-ok.wav` — marker voice (tasks 116/121)

- **What they are:** a spoken Norwegian **"Bra!"** for the BRA marker — the game's
  signature praise sound. PERFECT gets a brighter, slightly longer take; OK a softer,
  snappier one. Generated by
  [`scripts/gen-marker-voice.sh`](../../scripts/gen-marker-voice.sh): **espeak-ng**
  (Norwegian Bokmål female voice) → an ffmpeg warming chain (trim silence, slight
  pitch lift, warmth/air EQ, vocal presence, gentle de-robotize, loudness-normalise,
  fades). 16-bit mono PCM WAV, ~0.67 s / ~0.44 s.
- **License / likeness:** the audio is **machine-generated TTS**, not a human voice
  recording and **not an imitation of any identifiable person** — so there is no
  personality/likeness exposure (cf. [`tech-decisions.md` §4](../../.docs/tech-decisions.md)).
  Synthesized speech output is treated as **license-clean / public-domain** (espeak-ng
  claims no copyright over generated audio). Reproducible from the generator script.
- **Why they exist:** to give the marker a real spoken "Bra!" — an honest, warm
  placeholder that reads as the actual word, not an abstract chime. They are
  registered under the `PERFECT` / `OK` cues at bootstrap (off the tap path).
- **To be replaced by:** the real **Maren marker recording** — an owner/likeness-gated
  asset (see [`tech-decisions.md` §4](../../.docs/tech-decisions.md)) that **cannot be
  produced autonomously**. It is a **drop-in**: register the real clip under the same
  cue (or per-phrase `voice:<id>` keys) with **no call-site change**. These TTS takes
  are explicitly **not** Maren's line.

### Prior placeholder

An earlier synthesized **chime** (`bra-placeholder.wav`, not a voice) proved the
clip-playback pipeline end-to-end. It is superseded by the spoken takes above; the
pure-synth generator [`scripts/gen-voice-placeholder.mjs`](../../scripts/gen-voice-placeholder.mjs)
is retained for provenance and as a no-TTS-toolchain fallback.
